Before You Arrive

Questions & Answers

Everything you need to know before booking.

Where is the Pan Am Experience located?

+

The Pan Am Experience is located in Los Angeles, California. The full venue address and directions will be provided with your booking confirmation.

How long does the full experience take?

+

Plan for approximately three hours. You'll begin with cocktails at the Clipper Club, explore the museum and terminal, and then sit down for a five-course dinner aboard the 747. There's no rush. The evening is designed to unfold at its own pace.

Is there a dress code?

+

We suggest cocktail attire or smart casual. Many guests enjoy dressing for the era, and some arrive in full 1970s style. There's no strict requirement, but the experience feels best when you've dressed for it.

Can you accommodate dietary restrictions?

+

Yes. We accommodate vegetarian, vegan, gluten-free, and most allergy-related dietary needs. Please note your requirements when booking and our culinary team will prepare accordingly.

Is the experience suitable for children?

+

The experience is designed for adults and older teens. Children 12 and above are welcome when accompanied by an adult. The evening includes cocktail service and a multi-course dinner, so younger children may not enjoy the full duration.

Can I book for a private event or large group?

+

Absolutely. The Clipper Club, terminal, and 747 cabin are all available for private bookings. Corporate events, anniversaries, proposals, and milestone celebrations are our specialty.

Is parking available?

+

Yes. Free parking is available on-site. Details and directions are included in your booking confirmation email.

Our Story

Built by Hand.
Driven by Obsession.

The Pan Am Experience didn't start in a boardroom. It started in a garage in Los Angeles, with one man's refusal to let a golden era disappear.

Chapter One

Anthony's Garage

It started with a meal tray. Anthony Toth had been collecting Pan Am artifacts for years, quietly assembling what would become one of the most comprehensive private collections of airline memorabilia in the world. Original cabin panels. First-class china. Crew uniforms still pressed and pinned. The seats passengers sat in when the 747 was the largest commercial aircraft ever built.

The collection grew until it filled his garage in Los Angeles. Friends came to see it. Then friends of friends. People who'd flown Pan Am as children, who remembered the linen napkins and the meals served on real china, who wanted to feel that era one more time. Anthony realized something: the artifacts weren't enough. People didn't want to look at the past. They wanted to step inside it.

Chapter Two

The Gardena Years

The first Pan Am Experience opened in a warehouse in Gardena, California. It was modest by today's standards, but the ambition was anything but. Anthony and his team rebuilt an entire 747 first-class cabin from original parts. They sourced vintage travel posters, departure boards, and terminal signage. They trained a crew to serve dinner with the warmth and precision of a 1970s Pan Am flight.

Word spread quickly. What began as a passion project became a destination. ABC's 20/20 came to film. The Los Angeles Times, Atlas Obscura, and Smithsonian all covered the story. Guests flew in from across the country just for dinner. Every seating sold out. Every guest left with a story.

The Gardena space proved something important: there was a deep, genuine hunger for this kind of experience. Not a themed restaurant. Not a museum behind glass. An evening that actually transported you somewhere.

Chapter Three

The Citadel

The reimagined Pan Am Experience opens in summer 2026 at the Citadel in Los Angeles. It's the venue the experience always deserved. Four purpose-built spaces designed from the ground up: a 1970s terminal, the Clipper Club cocktail lounge, a full Pan Am history museum, and a meticulously restored Boeing 747 first-class cabin.

Every surface has been considered. The Eames chairs in the Clipper Club were sourced from a single supplier, each shell hand-finished in period-accurate colors. The pendant lights are inspired by George Nelson's Bubble Lamps, forty-two of them suspended at precise heights. The terminal walls use a custom-cut stone pattern drawn from Pan Am's original Worldport at JFK. Three weeks of installation for one texture that transports you the moment you walk in.

The 100th anniversary of Pan Am falls in 2027. The reimagined Experience opens just in time. What started in a garage is now the definitive celebration of the airline that connected the world.
